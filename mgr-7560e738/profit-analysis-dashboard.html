Accounting fixes applied: (1) GST excluded from revenue — GST collected is an IRAS liability, not income. Revenue = sub-total + bank fee only. (2) FX gain/loss column — for non-SGD jobs, the FX column shows the unrealised variance between the historical invoice-month rate and today's live rate. Green = FX gain, red = FX loss. "…" means historical rate is still loading. (3) Accounting mode toggle — Management view includes all confirmed jobs. Statutory (Accrual) view shows invoiced jobs only as recognised revenue; uninvoiced confirmed jobs appear in the WIP schedule as costs incurred against unrecognised revenue. (4) WIP schedule — (statutory mode) shows costs incurred on Customer PO / Vendor PO / DO/WCC jobs not yet billed. This is the balance sheet WIP figure your auditors will ask for. (5) Monthly break-even line — the dashed purple "Net after overhead" line shows gross profit minus monthly overhead per month. When this line is above zero, that month covered its overhead. Set your overhead costs in the panel above to activate.

Limitations to note for external reporting: OT rates are configured approximations — verify against actual payroll including CPF, leave provisions and overtime premiums. FX gain/loss is unrealised and computed at live rates; realised FX (actual bank conversion rate) requires payment-date FX records not currently stored. Period-end accruals and provisions are not computed. Consult your accountant before using these figures for statutory filing or tax purposes.
